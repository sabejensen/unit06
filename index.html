Members

Gerard Way

Known for his clean tenor on tracks like Welcome to the Black Parade, as well as his more unhinged and theatrical performances on tracks like Mama, Gerard Way is a versatile singer/songwriter and the band's creative driving force that gives MCR their unique sound and aesthetic.

Ray Toro

Often compared to the great Brian May, Ray Toro's guitar work elevates MCR above many contemporary punk bands. Oftentimes, especially in their earlier music, the group has no problem playing fast, messy punk riffs. But on tracks like S/C/A/R/E/C/R/O/W or Teenagers, Toro's classic rock roots really shine through with effortless, soaring solos.

Frank Iero

Frank Iero adds a level of raw energy and aggression that no one else in group matches. Especially known for his insane live performances, his punchy rhythm guitar is most potent on classic punk tracks like Vampire Money, or giving a new dimension to a ballad like The Light Behind Your Eyes.

Mikey Way

Gerard Way's little brother and one of the quieter contributing members of the band on bass, Mikey Way's influence can still be heavily felt across their discography, from the infectious riff on Planetary (GO), to the driving bass undercurrent on Give 'em Hell Kid.
